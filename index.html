Mono and Stereo

Mono (Monophonic)

Definition: Mono is a sound system that uses only one audio channel. All audio is combined into a single signal without spatial separation.

Characteristics:

  • Same sound in all speakers or headphones.
  • Does not provide directionality or depth.
  • Simpler and more cost-effective equipment.

Applications:

  • Old phones and radios.
  • Speeches and broadcasts focusing on clarity.

Stereo (Stereophonic)

Definition: Stereo uses two or more separate audio channels to create a sense of direction and depth in sound.

Characteristics:

  • Each channel can play different sounds, creating spatial perception.
  • Allows distinguishing where sounds are coming from.
  • Requires specific equipment, like two speakers.

Applications:

  • Music and movies for immersive experiences.
  • Games and modern sound systems.
  • Audio experiences with spatial awareness.
